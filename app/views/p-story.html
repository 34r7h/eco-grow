Story

Ecossentials has grown into Powell River’s Local Eco Market. For four and a half years now, Ecossentials has been providing Powell River with a wide variety of products that are organic, natural, ethical, sustainable, and of course local. We believe that these products being available is vital, not only for ourselves, but for our planet, our fellow human beings, our community, and our families. And there are so many other people who share the same values as us, and like us, these people want to use their money to support the products and producers that adhere to these same values. Each week there are more customers than the last. New people come in every day, saying, “I’ve never been here before, but so-n-so told me I had to come check it out.” Or often from visitors, “I’m so glad I happened to stumble upon you, I wish there was a store like this where I live!” Customers are always telling us how much they love and value having a resource like Ecossentials in Powell River. We as a community are so lucky to have the option of buying products that support everyone, from our many local produce farmer neighbours, to fair-trade organic cotton farmers in Egypt. Ecossentials is here to give all of us choices that we can feel proud of, products that support the kind of world we want to live in.

More and more, people are looking for the items they spend their money on to come from a good source. To buy housewares that are made by people who are paid and treated well, food that is as wholesome, nutritious and local as possible, cleaners that won’t pollute our ecosystem or bodies, clothing made with sustainable materials by people who are paid fairly for their work, toys that are fair-trade, plastic and battery-free, and to get it all with as little packaging and waste as possible.

Growing with our Community

Our business has doubled since this time last year. We can’t even fit all the things you want and ask us to carry in the store. It’s gotten to the point where we just can’t add any more products, even if customers are repeatedly requesting them. You’ve asked for so many things: natural make up, local meats, ethically made kids’ socks and underwear, cloth diapers, organic butter, gluten-free breads and crackers, more groceries. But we don’t have the space. We also can’t bring in another till or more scales to help when we’ve got customers lined up, because there’s just nowhere to put them. And forget being able to store all of our inventory, we have to keep much of it elsewhere, which makes stocking difficult and complicated. We’ve grown as much as we can in this space, and we’ve come to realize it’s time for something bigger.

And so, we’re moving to a larger space! We already have a building. It’s great, it’s close, and it’s got room for all the things we lack: ample storage and display space, a real office, a staff room. We’ll have more than enough room for 4 scales, and 2 tills, so when it’s busy there’ll be less waiting. We’ll have more freezer and cooler space, so we can share more local foods and produce. We’ll be able to carry more of the things that you’ve wanted so long for us to provide. We want to use this bigger space to continue to support the local people who produce things that we all want and need to buy, like fresh, organically-grown produce, and the local clothing and gifts that are so treasured. Many of the progressive members of Powell River’s community already meet or bump into each other here, and we want to encourage this. A very exciting part of this plan is that Carol Sigvaldason, of The Flying Yellow Breadbowl, is coming on board to open an organic cafeteria & take-out counter! Not only will this be a place to sit and meet, but Powell River will once again have access to her naturally delicious foods. The community has missed them, and we are proud to offer a venue for her to provide organic meals and snacks, as well as meals for you to take home and prepare. Quick, easy, and healthy.

We need to renovate the new space. There are walls that need to come down, and walls that need to go up. We also need to buy a lot of equipment; coolers and freezers and tills and scales are going to add up quick.
